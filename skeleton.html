  2. Elections Code Update

    posted by Trevor Smith, Senate Secretary on Oct. 24, 2014, 5:36 p.m.

    ASPC Senate has voted to change the elections code by an 11-1 vote. The changes state that the elections forum must happen on a Sunday. Elections will begin immediately after the forum and continue until 12:47pm on Tuesday. The full motion is below.

    “The following item will be added to the elections code as Section IV, Subsection k, Point 4: The elections forum will be held on a Sunday. The language of Section V, b, 3 will be changed to read: Polling shall begin immediately after the candidate’s forum. Polling shall continue until 12:47 PM the following Tuesday.”

  3. Elections Code Update

    posted by Trevor Smith, Senate Secretary on Oct. 3, 2014, 5:17 p.m.

    The following language, added in the Senate meeting of May 5, 2014, has been removed from the ASPC Elections Code by a 10-2 vote.

    Elections Code, Section IV, Subsection k. Point 3.

    “All candidates must run as individuals. Individuals shall not run as a group consisting of two or more candidates or run under a political party banner or heading. All advertising materials posted by the candidate (posters, candidate statement, Facebook descriptions, etc.) shall not endorse groups or political party candidates.”

  5. Vote by 9pm, Monday 9/23

    posted by Peter Hao Chen, Commissioner of Communications on Sept. 23, 2014, 11:43 a.m.

    Vote here

    Positions being elected include representatives for the Junior, Sophomore, and First Year classes as well as North & South Campus. Your voice is vital to our community.

    Here is a message from ASPC President Rachel Jackson:

    Support your peers by making a choice, engaging in campus discourse and shaping what Senate leadership looks like this year. Vote here at aspc.pomona.edu/vote/ by 9pm tonight.

    ASPC Senate works directly (directly, directly) with administration, faculty, and deans, and has a hand in shaping policy that affects you, the student body. North Campus and South Campus Representatives have incredible power, chairing both Food Committee and Residence Hall Committee, and sitting on the Student Affairs Committee. Class Presidents represent their class to administrators and on Senate and have funds to do class-wide programming. To read more about Senate duties, check out the Senate bylaws: http://aspc.pomona.edu/senate/policies/bylaws/

    A few issues Senate we will be looking at this year include alcohol policy, sexual violence culture, general education requirements, Dynamic of Difference and Power requirements, helping with the President's Strategic Plan for Diversity, and looking for ways to increase institutional memory. Senate does important work, so vote today and keep us accountable!

    It takes a community to speak truth to power. Vote today!
